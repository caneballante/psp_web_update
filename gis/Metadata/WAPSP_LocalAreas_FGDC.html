Description:
Abstract:
Local communities around the Puget Sound are working to integrate local efforts to advance the Puget Sound Partnership Action Agenda. Local governments, tribes, non-profit organizations, watershed, marine resource, and salmon recovery groups, interest groups, businesses, educational organizations, and citizens are collaborating to develop and coordinate local integrating organizations that foster implementation of Action Agenda priorities.To date, the PSP Leadership Council has recognized local integrating organizations (LIO) in nine of the ten Local Areas.The geographies of the LIOs were derived from the Puget Sound Partnership Action Areas, persuant to RCW 90.71.260.

Source_Contribution:
The statute creating the Puget Sound Puget Sound Partnership requires the creation of Action Areas to assist the Partnership in their work of restoring and rehabilitating the Puget Sound. These Action Areas boundaries represent geographic regions of the Puget Sound that have similar physical characteristics, upland linkages, and common interests where local restoration and rehabilitation plans can be developed and implemented. The seven areas were identified in legislation (RCW 90.71.260) as: The Strait of Juan de Fuca; San Juan Islands (renamed San Juan / Whatcom in Nov 2013); Whidbey Island (renamed Whidbey in Nov 2013); Hood Canal; North Central Puget Sound; South Central Puget Sound, and; South Puget Sound.
